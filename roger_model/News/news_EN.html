Spaceflight Mechanics and Exploration Systems Laboratory

News and Activities.

2021.11.22

IAC 2021 ①

Professor Tsuda attended this year’s IAC (International Astronomical Congress), held in Dubai. He was awarded the highest honor of “World Space Award”. With 1-minute speech at the opening ceremony, 15 minutes of presentation at the technical session, and an hour of lecture at the special session, the Hayabusa2 mission was prominently featured in the IAC-Dubai.
2021.11.22

IAC 2021 ②

Several students of Tsuda Laboratory were set to attend as well, but regrettably video-presented due to COVID-related restrictions. Professor Tsuda also visited the World Expo held in Dubai, as Hayabusa2 project cooperated in exhibitions of Expo’s Japanese pavilion.
2020.11.16

Book Publication

A book written by Prof. Tsuda has been published. The book depicts the thrill of the Hayabusa 2 project as it has been lived by the project members. It doesn't get any more real! (Kashioka)
2020.10.23

Drop Tower Experiment

I had a Drop Tower experiment in Hokkaido, Japan. The purpose is to investigate how sand behaves when it is sprayed with thrusters under microgravity. The results are very worth analyzing and I look forward to future research. (Yamakawa)
2020.08.03

Welcome Newcomer!

Ms. Kaoru Namiki has joined Tsuda Lab as a new student of SOKENDAI University this April. We have been having online meetings due to COVID19, but we will continue to work hard together with new members! (Kashioka)
2019.11.18

UKAREN 2019

We participated in the 63rd UKAREN at Tokushima. We discussed with the presenters from other fields and received questions and advice on my research through the presentations, which stimulated our research. (Yamakawa)
2019.10.28

IAC 2019

We attended IAC 2019 in Washington, D.C., Oct. 21-25. It was the 50th anniversary of Apollo 11 and the 70th anniversary of the convention, so the scale was off the charts! I was very nervous, but the presentation went well. (Ohira)
2019.05.20

Welcome Party

A new student has arrived at Tsuda Lab! Her name is Maiko Yamakawa. We had a party at Tsuda Lab, which served as a welcome party. The place is "Kiyaroya" in Fuchinobe. It's my personal favorite restaurant! The fish was absolutely good! (Ohira)
2018.10.05

IAC 2018

I attended IAC2018 in Bremen. The city of Bremen is famous for the Bremen Musicians, but it is also home to OHB, Germany's second largest space company, and there is a drop-test facility at a university near the venue. (Kashioka)
